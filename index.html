How to Broadcast for Sports

Assembly time: Personalized to everyone's skill and prep times
Awesome rating: 100% effective

Are you wondering how to become a sports reporter? This job is not only fulfilling but exhilarating, as well. This How-To outlines everything you need to know and do to succeed in the field of sports media and sports broadcast. Follow these steps and try it for yourself!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Camera
  • Microphone
  • Tripod
  • Research papers (Sports Statistics)
  • Personal Makeup
  • Outlines/Schedules
  • Water

  1. Research and prepare.

    Broadcasters and Reporters research information

    ➥ Research and gather all your information about the game/event.

    ➥ Plan out the different ideas for your content, photos, and videos.

    ➥ Prepare all needed materials like cameras, microphones, tripods, make-up, research papers, outlines, etc.

  2. Pre-game/pre-event.

    Charissa Thompson on the set of the Amazon Prime “Thursday Night Football” pregame show before an NFL football game

    ➥ Make sure you look the part by having appropriate clothing that is fashionable and flattering.

    ➥ Having a media presence means you must look physically attractive by having your hair and makeup done.

    ➥ Interact with fans, staff, and other reporters to gain more information about the event and game along with building your connections. It can also inspire more content ideas.

  3. Setting Up

    Sara Cardona Setting up for Live Broadcast

    ➥ Find a spot on the field or in the stadium with a suitable yet compelling background.

    ➥ Make sure the lighting is good and the environment is safe for you to broadcast freely.

    ➥ Touch up any makeup or redo your hair to make sure you a fully camera-ready.

    ➥ Go over notes and what information you want to broadcast.

    ➥ If you have an interview, make sure they are ready and with you for when you start rolling.

  4. Recording/On-air

    ➥ Make sure you are smiling and looking at the camera.

    ➥ Talk slowly, loudly, and confidently. Enunciate your words so the audience can understand you clearly.

  5. Clean up and Finisher

    Charissa Thompson interacting and interview fans.

    Cleaning up and leaving a memorable presence is just as important as the broadcast and reporting itself.

    ➥ Clean up your set up station and leave it cleaner than before you got there.

    ➥ Stay for post-game: Interact with the fans, players, faculty, coaches, and administration to create connections and retrieve more information.
